# The Shakti Sankalpa — Direction B Print Spread Layout

**Book number:** 04  
**Title:** The Shakti Sankalpa  
**Subtitle:** A 40-Day Devotional Guide to Purpose, Desire, Liberation, and the Goddess Within  
**Author:** Sheetal Kandola  
**Series mark:** A Shakti Shala Temple Library Guide  
**Direction:** B — Devotional Editorial  
**Use:** Front cover + spine + back cover composition for visual generation and Sheetal approval

---

## Spread Order

For a full wraparound book cover, design left to right:

```text
BACK COVER | SPINE | FRONT COVER
```

The full spread should look like a single sacred object, not three disconnected panels.

---

# FRONT COVER — Filled Layout

## Front Cover Visual

Right panel.

A dark stone inner temple chamber. The chamber is intimate, not huge. Candlelight at the lower edges. Deep burgundy silk or cloth appears subtly near the base or side, like ritual fabric. The walls carry faint yantra carvings, not loud symbols. At the center is the golden womb / cosmic egg, glowing softly from within.

The golden form should feel like:

```text
Hiranyagarbha
Golden Womb
cosmic seed
vow gestating
truth before language
```

It should not look like anatomy.

## Front Cover Text Placement

**Top small series mark:**

```text
A SHAKTI SHALA
TEMPLE LIBRARY GUIDE
```

**Main title:**

```text
THE
SHAKTI
SANKALPA
```

Recommended title treatment:
- THE = smaller
- SHAKTI = large
- SANKALPA = large, slightly wider tracking
- warm ivory and muted gold mix

**Subtitle:**

```text
A 40-Day Devotional Guide
to Purpose, Desire, Liberation,
and the Goddess Within
```

**Optional pull-line near lower third:**

```text
A vow made from truth.
```

**Author at bottom:**

```text
SHEETAL KANDOLA
```

**Small bottom mark:**

```text
gold yantra / Shakti Shala mark / restrained ornament
```

# SPINE — Filled Layout

## Spine Visual

Narrow center panel. Dark black stone texture with a soft vertical ember-gold line, as if the vow is running through the spine of the book.

Use restrained gold ornaments at top and bottom.

## Spine Text Option A — Vertical

```text
THE SHAKTI SANKALPA
```

Then smaller:

```text
SHEETAL KANDOLA
```

At the base:

```text
SHAKTI SHALA
```

## Spine Text Option B — Stacked

```text
THE
SHAKTI
SANKALPA
```

Small divider.

```text
SHEETAL
KANDOLA
```

Small divider.

```text
TEMPLE
LIBRARY
```

## Spine Notes

Keep spine simple. No crowded subtitle. No large image. The spine should feel like the book’s energetic axis.

# BACK COVER — Filled Layout

## Back Cover Visual

Left panel.

Continue the same dark temple chamber, but quieter and more spacious than the front. The back cover should have a readable text field. Use subtle candlelight, faint wall carvings, and a dim golden glow at the lower center or far background.

The back cover should feel like:

```text
a quiet temple wall
a vow after it has been spoken
practice beginning
stillness after invocation
```

## Back Cover Text Placement

**Top small mark:**

```text
gold Shakti Shala / yantra ornament
```

**Opening pull quote:**

```text
Sankalpa is not a wish.
```

**Body copy:**

```text
It is not an affirmation
or a performance of becoming.

It is a vow made from truth —
a commitment that arises
from the deeper Self
and asks to be lived.

The Shakti Sankalpa guides you
through witnessing, the Golden Womb,
the four aims of human life,
and goddess-linked reflection practices
for dharma, artha, kama, and moksha.

This is a 40-day devotional guide
for listening beneath the noise
and letting the vow move
from words into bone.
```

**CTA / closing line:**

```text
Begin the 40-day Sankalpa practice.
```

**Footer:**

```text
SHAKTI SHALA
TEMPLE LIBRARY
```

## Back Cover Typography Notes

- Use warm ivory for body copy.
- Use muted gold for the opening pull quote and CTA.
- Keep line spacing generous.
- Do not overload the back cover.
- Copy should be centered or slightly above center.
