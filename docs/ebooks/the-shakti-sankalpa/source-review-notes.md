# 2. PDF review

The PDF is a 15-page visual prototype of **The Shakti Sankalpa**.

It is no longer only a rough e-book idea. It has become a visual Temple Library prototype.

## Visual thesis

The design successfully carries:

- dark sanctuary atmosphere
- golden womb imagery
- ember/gold glow
- devotional editorial tone
- structured teaching progression
- goddess correspondences
- 40-day practice architecture

The strongest visual throughline is:

```text
Sankalpa as gestated vow, not manufactured goal.
```

## Page-by-page notes

### Page 1 — Cover

Title:

```text
The Shakti Sankalpa
```

Subtitle:

```text
A 40-Day Devotional Guide to Purpose, Desire, Liberation, and the Goddess Within.
```

Notes:

- Strong cover concept.
- Golden womb / cosmic egg image is aligned with Hiranyagarbha.
- Typography feels premium and devotional.
- The period after “Within.” may be removed for cleaner cover design.
- Add/standardize series line later: “From the Shakti Shala Temple Library.”

### Page 2 — Opening Invocation

Notes:

- Strong opening frame: “Before Sankalpa is spoken, the field is prepared.”
- Needs Sanskrit and translation review.
- The line “may You accept the oblations of the devotee” should be reviewed for accuracy and elegance.
- Confirm whether mantra transliterations should use diacritics or simplified readable Sanskrit.

### Page 3 — Before You Create Anything, Create Yourself

Notes:

- Strong opening chapter image and concept.
- The diagram comparing surface self and deeper self works well.
- Preserve the line: “The first thing to create before you create anything else is yourself.”
- This page should become Chapter 1.

### Page 4 — Atma Tattva Avalokanam

Notes:

- The four-step descent works well visually.
- Standardize spelling: Atma Tattva Avalokanam / Ātma Tattva Avalokanam depending Sheetal preference.
- “Parigrahas” should be confirmed.
- This page should become a full guided practice, not only a conceptual slide.

### Page 5 — Hiranyagarbha: The Golden Womb

Notes:

- This is one of the strongest pages.
- Preserve the line: “Your Sankalpa is not a mental sentence. It is not manufactured by the mind. It is gestated in the womb of consciousness.”
- Golden womb image should be central to the e-book cover system.
- This page defines the visual identity of the whole Sankalpa guide.

### Page 6 — What Sankalpa Really Is

Notes:

- Strong comparison table: Goal / Wish / Affirmation / Sankalpa.
- Keep this table, but refine language carefully.
- “Outcome: Reorganization around Truth (Nondualistic)” is strong.
- Confirm Sanskrit root explanation: Sat + Kalpa.

### Page 7 — How to Hear Your Sankalpa

Notes:

- Strong funnel/cone visual for listening inward.
- Keep the three-step process:
  - Śravaṇa / hearing the signal
  - Manana / creating the inner container
  - Nididhyāsana / embodying the instruction
- The bottom rule is important: “It must be stated in the affirmative present tense.”
- Confirm “perfect resolve is a resolve without doubt.”

### Page 8 — Purusharthas Map

Notes:

- Strong integrated life map.
- Quote should be reviewed/cited. It may be commonly attributed to Gospel of Thomas; do not present as verified unless confirmed.
- Good framing: “The vow does not float above life. It must touch money, desire, responsibility, and liberation.”

### Page 9 — Dharma and Tripura Bhairavi

Notes:

- Strong teaching: Bhairavi as tapas, discipline, devotion, and courage.
- Journal prompt is strong.
- Embodiment practice is clear.
- Goddess image should be reviewed for lineage sensitivity and source/usage rights.

### Page 10 — Artha and Maa Kamala

Notes:

- Strong framing: prosperity aligned with dharma, not greed.
- “Artha is not the rejection of spirit; it is the support structure that allows life and service to be held with dignity.” This should be expanded in the manuscript.
- Image rights/lineage sensitivity review needed.

### Page 11 — Kama and Tripura Sundari

Notes:

- Strong but needs careful tone.
- Avoid cheap tantra or oversexualized language.
- Keep “sacred desire when aligned with dharma.”
- Embodiment practice should be framed gently and with consent/choice.

### Page 12 — Moksha and Maa Kali

Notes:

- Emotional peak of the prototype.
- Strong prompt: “What am I ready to let die so I can be reborn as who I truly am?”
- Kali imagery is intense; confirm use with Sheetal.
- Include care note around catharsis and integration.

### Page 13 — Chinnamasta and the Surrender of Control

Notes:

- This is the most sensitive page.
- Strong teaching as ego surrender / divine will.
- Because imagery is intense, this may need to be:
  - community-gated,
  - framed as advanced,
  - or softened visually for open-gate use.
- Sheetal must approve public-facing treatment.

### Page 14 — The 9 Hidden Nights and the 40-Day Vow

Notes:

- Strong bridge from Gupt Navratri to 40-day devotion.
- Keep: “The 9 hidden nights are not for performance.”
- Strong line: “40 days to anchor your Sankalpa in your bones.”
- This page should become the bridge into the tracker/practice back matter.

### Page 15 — Integration Rhythm & The Next Doorway

Notes:

- Strong closing page.
- The next doorway copy is strategically important.
- It correctly positions the text as a Temple Library artifact, not a normal workbook.
- Suggested addition: a “Day 41” reflection based on the companion transcript.

# 5. Corrections and review flags

## Name corrections

- Sheetal Candola → Sheetal Kandola

## Title corrections

- H-A-T-I Sankalpa → Shakti Sankalpa

## Sanskrit / transliteration review

Confirm preferred spelling for:

- Sankalpa
- Ātma / Atma
- Tattva / Tatva
- Avalokanam / Avalakanam
- Hiranyagarbha
- Purushartha / Purusharthas
- Śravaṇa / Sravana
- Manana
- Nididhyāsana / Nididhyasana
- Gupt Navratri / Gupta Navratri
- Parigraha / Parigrahas
- Daśa Mahāvidyā / Dasa Mahavidya

## Goddess names to standardize

- Tripura Bhairavi
- Maa Kamala
- Tripura Sundari / Shodashi
- Maa Kali
- Maa Chinnamasta

## Claims to review

- “40 days rewrites neural pathways” should be softened unless Sheetal wants to cite neuroscience.
- “Signal to the vagus nerve” should be softened unless clinically grounded.
- “Discharge trapped adrenaline and cortisol” should be softened or reviewed.
- Avoid overclaiming therapeutic or neurological effects.

Suggested safer language:

```text
The practice can help the body feel more grounded and receptive.
```

instead of:

```text
This rewrites the nervous system.
```

## Imagery sensitivity

Review public use of:

- Kali with weapons/skulls
- Chinnamasta severed-head imagery
- goddess AI-generated images
- any sensualized Tripura Sundari visuals

Recommendation:

For open-gate/public e-book, use more symbolic temple art, yantra, flame, womb, moon, river, or abstract goddess-presence visuals unless Sheetal explicitly approves deity images.

# 6. Manuscript expansion recommendations

## Expand from 15 pages to 35–45 pages

Current PDF works as visual prototype.

Final e-book should include:

- chapter introductions
- guided practices
- reflective pauses
- journal pages
- 40-day tracker
- glossary
- lineage/source notes
- practice care notes
- next doorway into Shakti Shala

## Each chapter should include

```text
1. Teaching
2. Why it matters
3. Reflection prompt
4. Embodiment option
5. Sankalpa integration
6. Gentle care note where needed
```

## Add care language

Especially for:

- Kali catharsis
- Chinnamasta imagery
- tantric breath
- self-touch / sensuality practices
- shadow inquiry

Care language should preserve agency:

```text
Move slowly.
Choose what feels appropriate.
Pause if your system feels overwhelmed.
This guide is a practice companion, not a substitute for therapeutic or crisis support.
```
