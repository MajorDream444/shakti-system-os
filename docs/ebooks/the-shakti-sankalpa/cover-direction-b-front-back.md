## Purpose

This cover should feel like a **Temple Library artifact**, not a generic spiritual e-book cover.

It should communicate:

- vow
- devotion
- golden womb
- inner witnessing
- Sankalpa as truth, not wish
- forty-day practice
- Shakti Shala as sanctuary

The source teaching positions Sankalpa as a vow made from truth, not an affirmation or intention. The cover should visually hold the idea:

> Your Sankalpa is not manufactured.  
> It is gestated.

## Visual Thesis

A dark devotional temple chamber holds a luminous golden womb / cosmic egg at the center.

The egg is not decorative. It is the living symbol of Hiranyagarbha — the Golden Womb — the source from which Sankalpa is gestated.

The scene should feel sacred, editorial, restrained, and atmospheric.

No literal goddess figure is needed.

No fantasy goddess poster.

No Canva wellness design.

No neon tantra.

## FRONT COVER PROMPT

```text
Create a premium 6x9 portrait book cover, front cover only, for a sacred devotional e-book titled “The Shakti Sankalpa” by Sheetal Kandola.

Design direction: Direction B — Devotional Editorial. More sacred, atmospheric, ritual, and refined than a public-facing guide, but still restrained, elegant, and lineage-sensitive.

Scene: an intimate dark stone temple chamber at night, carved Indian/Himalayan sanctuary architecture, candlelight, deep burgundy silk, black stone, faint incense smoke, subtle yantra engravings in the walls, and a luminous golden womb / cosmic egg suspended or resting at the center of the chamber. The golden womb should feel like Hiranyagarbha, the source of creation, not a literal biological image. It should glow from within with soft ember-gold light, delicate sacred geometry, and subtle flowing current lines.

Mood: devotional, quiet, powerful, feminine but not soft-only, sacred but modern, premium editorial, emotionally intelligent, not fantasy, not horror, not over-mystical.

Composition: strong central focal architecture. The golden womb / cosmic egg is centered in the lower-middle or middle third, surrounded by temple shadows and candlelight. Leave clean negative space in the upper third for title text. The cover should feel like entering an inner chamber of vow and practice.

Typography direction: elegant high-contrast serif title in warm ivory and muted gold. Small uppercase series mark at top: “A SHAKTI SHALA TEMPLE LIBRARY GUIDE.” Large title: “THE SHAKTI SANKALPA.” Subtitle below in refined serif or quiet italic: “A 40-Day Devotional Guide to Purpose, Desire, Liberation, and the Goddess Within.” Author name at bottom: “SHEETAL KANDOLA.”

Color palette: obsidian black, temple charcoal, deep burgundy, ember gold, muted antique gold, ash ivory, warm stone, subtle clay red.

Include small gold ornamental dividers or sacred geometry accents only if restrained and elegant.

Avoid: generic lotus clichés, neon colors, fantasy goddess illustration, oversexualized imagery, literal womb anatomy, stock meditation woman, chakra rainbow, AI fantasy excess, cluttered text, cheap tantra aesthetic, motivational self-help cover.

The cover must feel like a sacred Temple Library text, not a lead magnet.
```

## BACK COVER PROMPT

```text
Create the matching back cover for a premium 6x9 devotional e-book titled “The Shakti Sankalpa” by Sheetal Kandola.

Design direction: Direction B — Devotional Editorial. Sacred, atmospheric, restrained, and readable.

Scene: same dark temple chamber world as the front cover, but quieter and more spacious. Use black stone, subtle carved patterns, soft candlelight, burgundy fabric, faint incense, and a dim golden glow suggesting the same golden womb / cosmic egg without making it the main focal point. The back cover should feel like the quiet wall of a temple chamber after the vow has been spoken.

Composition: provide a centered readable text area with enough dark negative space for back-cover copy. Keep the visual atmosphere rich but not busy. Use soft vignette, subtle gold ornaments, and small sacred geometry mark near the top or bottom.

Back cover copy:

“Sankalpa is not a wish.

It is not an affirmation or a performance of becoming.

It is a vow made from truth — a commitment that arises from the deeper Self and asks to be lived.

The Shakti Sankalpa guides you through witnessing, the Golden Womb, the four aims of human life, and goddess-linked reflection practices for dharma, artha, kama, and moksha.

This is a 40-day devotional guide for listening beneath the noise and letting the vow move from words into bone.

Begin the 40-day Sankalpa practice.”

Footer: “SHAKTI SHALA TEMPLE LIBRARY”

Typography: refined serif and humanist sans pairing, warm ivory text with muted gold emphasis. Keep line spacing generous. Text must be legible.

Avoid: crowded text, overly bright glow, fantasy goddess imagery, literal womb anatomy, stock spiritual symbols, generic wellness design, neon tantra, chakra rainbow, overdramatic mystical effects.
```
