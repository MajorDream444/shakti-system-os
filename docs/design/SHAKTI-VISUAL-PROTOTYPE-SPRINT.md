# Shakti Visual Prototype Sprint

## Purpose

This sprint does not redesign Sheetal Kandola as a person. Sheetal is the brand.

The work is to identify, refine, and systemize the visual and operational signals already present in her work so her team can produce consistently, seekers can recognize the world they are entering, and the business can support recurring containers, full retreats, and dependable revenue.

> Preserve the medicine. Refine the invitation. Systemize the expression.

## Logo Doctrine

The current Kali emblem should be treated as a **sacred seal**, not a decorative stamp.

### Logo hierarchy

1. **Full Seal** — covers, ceremonial invitations, opening/closing deck slides, retreat materials, certificates, and major brand moments.
2. **Reduced Mark** — future simplified eye/flame/bindi symbol for avatars, favicons, small signatures, watermarks, and interface icons.
3. **Wordmark** — `Sheetal Kandola` or `Shakti Shala` in the approved serif when the full seal would be too heavy.
4. **Ghost Mark** — seal at 3–8% opacity as emboss, corner watermark, or tonal texture.

### Avoid

- full logo on every carousel slide
- casual recoloring
- stretching or cropping
- loud placement over photography
- glow, bevel, metallic, or novelty spiritual effects

## Typography

- Display: Cormorant Garamond, Canela, or comparable refined serif
- Body: Inter, Manrope, or comparable humanist sans
- Doctrine labels: restrained small caps or serif italics
- Never use faux-Indian novelty fonts

## Success Standard

Success is not that every post looks identical.

Success is that every expression feels like it came from the same sanctuary.

The public should recognize Shakti Shala before seeing the logo. The team should know how to create without asking Sheetal to reinvent the vision each time. The operating system should help Sheetal invite aligned women, fill retreats responsibly, receive payment clearly, and hand repeatable work to her team.
