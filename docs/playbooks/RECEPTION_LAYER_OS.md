# Reception Layer OS

## Status

Reusable MAIM / AMA MOB operating-system pattern.

This document defines the standard Reception Layer for Living Intelligence OS builds, including Shakti System OS, MAIM, Theo, Flo, and future client ecosystems.

---

# Core Thesis

Every Living Intelligence OS needs a receptionist before it needs more features.

The receptionist is not a chatbot.
The receptionist is not the practitioner.
The receptionist is not pretending to be human.

The receptionist is a listening layer that protects the practitioner, welcomes the visitor, gathers context, and prepares the right pathway.

```text
Discovery
→ Reception Layer
→ Portal
→ Intake / Begin
→ Sanctuary
→ Vault
→ Practitioner Handoff
```

---

# Purpose

The Reception Layer exists to:

1. protect the practitioner's time
2. let visitors speak naturally instead of forcing them into forms
3. collect richer client data through voice, SMS, WhatsApp, Telegram, web chat, and phone
4. identify readiness, urgency, intent, and pathway fit
5. prepare structured handoff notes before a human conversation
6. improve the Living Vault and operational intelligence over time

The practitioner should remain at the top of the mountain.

The receptionist gathers the terrain before the practitioner steps in.

---

# Why Voice Matters

Typed forms lose context.

A person's internal experience changes as it moves from thought to fingers to typed response.

Voice captures:

```text
emotion
hesitation
urgency
cadence
story
memory
confusion
excitement
language patterns
```

For this reason, every MAIM / AMA MOB client system should treat voice as a first-class intake channel.

Voice does not replace forms.
Voice enriches them.

# Standard Channels

Each implementation may use different tools, but the Reception Layer should be designed for:

```text
Website voice widget
Website chat
Phone number
SMS
WhatsApp
Telegram
Instagram DM
Email
Form fallback
```

Initial deployments may start with one or two channels, but the architecture should assume multi-channel expansion.

---

# Standard Responsibilities

The receptionist may:

```text
greet the visitor
explain its role
collect the visitor's story
ask clarifying questions
capture contact details
identify goals and pain points
identify urgency
identify readiness
recommend a starting pathway
book or request a human conversation
summarize the interaction
create or update CRM records
route the visitor into the right next step
```

The receptionist should not:

```text
pretend to be the practitioner
claim human authority
diagnose medical, legal, financial, or psychological conditions
make promises of transformation
pressure the visitor to buy
replace human discernment
publish private reflections without approval
```

# Journey Stages

The receptionist should adapt based on relationship stage.

```text
Visitor
Explorer
Applicant
Seeker
Member
Practitioner
Retreat Participant
Alumni

```

A first-time visitor needs orientation.
A returning seeker needs continuity.
A retreat applicant needs preparation.
A member needs rhythm.

---

# Practitioner Handoff

Before a human call, the system should prepare:

```text
Who this person is
Why they came
What they said in their own words
What they have tried
What they are asking for
What they seem ready for
What needs caution
Recommended next pathway
Suggested human conversation focus
```

This lets the practitioner begin at hour three instead of hour zero.

# Relationship to Tools

Tools are adapters, not the architecture.

Possible tools:

```text
ElevenLabs for voice agents
Twilio for phone / SMS
WhatsApp Business API
Telegram bot API
OpenRouter for model routing
Google Calendar / Cal.com for booking
Airtable for CRM and readiness tracking
Google Drive for source documents
Graphify for relationship context
Notion for team operations
```

No single vendor should become the operating system.

The Reception Layer must remain portable.

---

# Sovereignty Standard

Every implementation should avoid unnecessary lock-in.

Minimum standard:

```text
exportable conversation logs
exportable transcripts
exportable summaries
clear data ownership
documented API keys
documented providers
fallback manual workflow
human override
```

If a vendor is replaced, the client relationship memory should survive.

# Standard Build Sequence

```text
1. Define role and name
2. Define guardrails
3. Define intake questions
4. Define escalation rules
5. Define CRM fields
6. Define calendar / booking behavior
7. Connect initial channel
8. Test with realistic conversations
9. Review transcripts
10. Tighten prompt and knowledge base
11. Connect to Airtable / Notion / Drive
12. Add phone / WhatsApp / Telegram after the core flow works
```

---

# Human Approval Gates

Escalate to a human when:

```text
high emotional distress appears
medical / legal / financial advice is requested
retreat readiness is unclear
payment issue arises
conflict or complaint appears
the person asks for the practitioner directly
AI confidence is low
private or sensitive story needs care
```

---

# Data Outputs

Every conversation should produce:

```text
Raw transcript
Short summary
Structured profile updates
Readiness assessment
Potential pathway
Action items
Human review flag
Next recommended step
```

The raw transcript belongs in the Vault/intake archive.
The structured fields belong in Airtable.
The human action item belongs in Notion or task management.
The relationship graph belongs in Graphify.

---

# Operating Principle

```text
The receptionist should make the human relationship more prepared, not less human.
```

Use automation to create better context, better timing, and better stewardship.

Never use it to fake intimacy or replace the practitioner.
