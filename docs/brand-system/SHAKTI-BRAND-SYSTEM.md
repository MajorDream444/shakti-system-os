# Shakti Brand System

**Status:** Working source of truth  
**System owner:** Shakti System OS  
**Primary use:** Social media, decks, landing experiences, e-books, ceremonies, retreat materials, and future team handoff

## 1. Core Principle

Shakti Shala must not appear as a collection of unrelated posts, decks, ceremonies, and offers.

It should feel like one living sanctuary expressed through multiple formats.

```text
Natural sanctuary on the surface.
Precision architecture beneath.
```

The medicine remains relational, embodied, lineage-sensitive, and human-led. The design system exists to protect recognition, coherence, and trust around that medicine.

## 2. Brand Architecture

### Public-facing sanctuary

**Shakti Shala**

The place women enter, practice, gather, and return.

### Internal operating layer

**Shakti System OS**

The doctrine, pathways, readiness logic, content system, CRM, retreat pipeline, and handoff infrastructure beneath the public experience.

### Core body of work

**Shakti Shadow & Somatics**

The central method and teaching field integrating Shakti Tantra, somatics, shadow integration, nervous-system literacy, diaspora identity, and feminine sovereignty.

## 3. Visual North Star

The visual field should feel:

- dark, feminine, devotional, and inward
- premium rather than decorative
- spacious rather than dense
- cinematic rather than flashy
- sacred without becoming fantasy
- emotionally grounded rather than performatively mystical

The consistent emotional signal is:

```text
There is depth here.
There is structure here.
You do not need to perform here.
```

## 5. Typography

### Display

Use a refined serif such as Cormorant Garamond, Canela, or a comparable editorial face.

Use for:

- cover headlines
- carousel opening statements
- deck section dividers
- threshold language
- selected pull quotes

### Body

Use a clean humanist sans-serif such as Inter or Manrope.

Use for:

- teaching copy
- practical guidance
- captions
- slide explanations
- CTA language

### Rule

Poetic language may open the door. Clear language must guide the next step.

## 6. Image Doctrine

### Use

- moonlight, candlelight, diyas, dusk, smoke, old stone
- Himalayan atmosphere, forests, rivers, temples, mountains
- mala beads, subtle sacred geometry, handwritten diagrams
- archival devotional illustration used with context and permission
- bodies in grounded, non-performative practice
- restrained symbolic references to the Mahavidyas
- fire and water as balancing currents

### Avoid

- generic stock wellness imagery
- hypersexualized tantra visuals
- fantasy goddess portraits presented as doctrine
- overloaded mandalas and spiritual collage
- bright boho retreat aesthetics
- AI-generated sacred diagrams presented as authoritative sources
- cultural symbols used without explanation

### Sacred-image rule

Traditional diagrams and deity imagery must be reviewed for accuracy, attribution, context, and appropriateness before publication. AI may assist with layout or restoration, but must not silently rewrite lineage material.

## 7. Content Families

Every post should belong to a recognizable visual and doctrinal family.

### A. Doctrine / Teaching

Purpose: clarify Shakti Tantra, somatics, shadow, nervous-system literacy, and lineage.

Visual language: archival diagram, warm cream paper, charcoal field, precise labels, minimal ornament.

### B. Moon Rhythm

Purpose: orient women around New Moon and Full Moon practice.

Visual language: dark sky, subtle lunar disc, reflected water, soft gold linework, ritual spaciousness.

### C. Goddess Pathways

Purpose: introduce one goddess, teaching, shadow, or practice with care.

Visual language: one symbolic object or atmosphere per post; avoid literal AI goddess spectacle.

### D. Somatic Practice

Purpose: offer grounded embodiment guidance.

Visual language: close body detail, breath, hands, spine, feet, movement, warm natural light.

### E. Founder / Origin / Lineage Story

Purpose: reveal Sheetal's authority, journey, cultural context, and why the work exists.

Visual language: documentary photography, place, archive, field notes, restrained editorial captions.

### F. Community / Invitation

Purpose: invite women into a circle, ceremony, self-audit, container, or retreat pathway.

Visual language: threshold, gathering, path, doorway, warm interior light. Never urgency-heavy.

## 8. Carousel System

### Standard structure

1. **Threshold:** one strong emotional or doctrinal statement
2. **Orientation:** name the context clearly
3. **Teaching:** explain the principle
4. **Embodied relevance:** connect it to the body or lived experience
5. **Reflection:** invite observation without diagnosis
6. **Practice:** offer one grounded action
7. **Integration:** explain what to carry forward
8. **Invitation:** save, reflect, attend, or begin the next doorway

Not every carousel needs ten slides. Use the smallest number that preserves clarity.

### Slide rules

- one emotional job per slide
- no more than one main idea per slide
- use generous margins
- keep paragraphs short
- reserve serif type for emphasis, not every sentence
- allow images to breathe
- place citations or source notes where doctrine requires them
- CTA should feel like continuation, not conversion pressure

## 9. New Moon / Full Moon Design Grammar

### New Moon

Emotional field: inward, quiet, listening, gestation, release of noise.

Primary colors: obsidian, burgundy, charcoal, muted gold.

Primary motifs: dark moon, seed, womb, doorway, still water, candle, closed flower.

### Full Moon

Emotional field: illumination, witnessing, integration, completion, release.

Primary colors: moon ivory, charcoal, deep blue-gray, muted gold, ember detail.

Primary motifs: illuminated path, reflective water, open flower, altar, mountain light.

### Shared rule

Moon content must connect lunar symbolism to practice, embodiment, or doctrine. It should not read like generic astrology content.

## 10. Pitch Deck System

All Shakti decks should use the same visual grammar as the public sanctuary.

### Deck sequence

1. Cover / thesis
2. Why this work exists
3. The problem or threshold women face
4. Sheetal's authority and body of work
5. The Shakti Shala ecosystem
6. Pathways and containers
7. Community rhythm
8. Retreat / sanctuary vision
9. Operating structure and stewardship
10. Invitation / next decision

### Deck rules

- one major claim per slide
- high image-to-text discipline
- use diagrams only when they clarify a real relationship
- no generic startup metrics aesthetic
- no excessive icons
- use full-bleed imagery sparingly and intentionally
- preserve human review on doctrine, lineage, and claims

## 11. Social Team Governance

### Approval rule

```text
AI may draft.
The team may refine.
Sheetal approves doctrine-sensitive material.
```

### Before publishing, confirm

- Is the language accurate to Sheetal's work?
- Is the visual culturally and spiritually appropriate?
- Does the post belong to a defined content family?
- Is the next step clear without pressure?
- Does this feel like Shakti Shala, or merely a spiritual post?
- Is the source image licensed, original, or properly attributed?
- Has any Sanskrit, deity, ritual, or lineage reference been reviewed?
