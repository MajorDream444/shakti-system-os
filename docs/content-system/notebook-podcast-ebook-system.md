# Notebook Podcast-to-Ebook System

**Project:** Shakti System OS  
**Client:** Sheetal Kandola / Shakti Shala  
**System layer:** Temple Library, doctrine preservation, content architecture, e-book production  
**Status:** Active workflow

---

## Purpose

This workflow turns source material into grounded audio reflection, then into e-book chapters, then into Temple Library artifacts.

The goal is not to manufacture content quickly. The goal is to preserve Sheetal's doctrine, voice, lineage sensitivity, and emotional precision while creating usable Shakti Shala assets.

This system supports the larger Shakti System OS sequence:

```text
Doctrine
→ Positioning
→ Front Door
→ Self-Audit / Lead Capture
→ Community Rhythm
→ Container Pathway
→ Retreat Pipeline
→ Sanctuary / Shakti Fest Vision
```

The podcast layer gives Sheetal a way to hear the story beneath the work before every e-book is finalized.

---

## Core thesis

The e-book series is not a collection of random PDFs.

It is the reader's path into Shakti Shala.

```text
A woman arrives seeking clarity.
She does not need to be sold.
She needs orientation.

She learns that transformation begins with rhythm.
Then she meets the Goddess as pathway, not decoration.
Then she learns Sankalpa as vow, not affirmation.
Then she meets shadow with care.
Then, if aligned, she approaches retreat with readiness.
```

The podcast skill exists to reveal that story before the written artifact is overbuilt.

---

## What this workflow protects

This workflow protects against:

- turning Sheetal's teachings into generic wellness copy
- flattening Tantra, goddess practice, or shadow work into aesthetic language
- creating e-books that feel like lead magnets instead of Temple Library texts
- rushing into writing before the doctrine is clear
- losing the emotional arc of the source material
- skipping human review where lineage precision matters

The governing principle remains:

```text
We are not changing the medicine.
We are building the structure around it.
```

## The four-layer extraction

After every podcast episode, extract four layers.

### 1. Emotional Layer

What does the reader feel?

Examples:

- uncertainty
- longing
- overwhelm
- readiness
- grief
- desire
- devotion
- fear of intensity
- need for rhythm

### 2. Doctrine Layer

What principle is being preserved?

Examples:

- Sankalpa is vow, not wish
- rhythm comes before intensity
- the Goddess is pathway, not decoration
- retreat requires readiness
- shadow is not failure
- structure protects the medicine

### 3. Operational Layer

Where does this fit in the Shakti System?

Examples:

- front door
- self-audit
- weekly circle
- Temple Library
- 6-week container
- retreat readiness pathway
- content vault
- onboarding sequence

### 4. Mythology Layer

What symbols, goddesses, places, or images are active?

Examples:

- temple doorway
- mountain path
- golden womb
- moon rhythm
- Kali descent
- Durga foundation
- river/current
- inner chamber

## NotebookLM master prompt

Use this reusable prompt for each e-book episode.

```text
Create a source-grounded podcast-style narrative episode for the Shakti Shala e-book series.

The purpose is not to summarize the source material mechanically. The purpose is to reveal the deeper story, doctrine, emotional arc, and reader pathway that should become an e-book.

Use a calm, precise, devotional, lineage-sensitive tone.

Do not use generic wellness language.
Do not make this sound like a sales funnel.
Do not over-mysticize the material.
Do not flatten Tantra, goddess practice, or shadow work into aesthetic language.

Structure the episode as:

1. Opening scene / doorway
2. What this source material is really about
3. The emotional problem the reader is carrying
4. The doctrine beneath the teaching
5. The transformation arc
6. The chapter spine for the e-book
7. The practice or reflection that belongs inside
8. The next doorway in Shakti Shala

The output should feel like a reflective audio essay that Sheetal could listen to and say:
"Yes. That is the story beneath this work."
```

## Review rules

Every e-book generated through this workflow needs human review before publication.

Sheetal should review:

- Sanskrit transliteration
- mantra translations
- goddess framing
- lineage sensitivity
- tone and voice
- whether the asset should be public, community-gated, or container-only
- whether any advanced practices need care notes

Major / operator should review:

- system fit
- CTA alignment
- Airtable/Notion/GitHub capture
- e-book pathway role
- handoff documentation
- future team usability

- CTAs feeling manipulative
